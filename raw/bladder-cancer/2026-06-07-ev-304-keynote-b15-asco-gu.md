# KEYNOTE-B15 / EV-304 — Perioperative EV+pembro vs GC in Cisplatin-Eligible MIBC

**Source URL:** https://www.asco.org/abstracts-presentations/216863  
**Also:** https://www.pfizer.com/news/press-release/press-release-detail/padcevtm-keytrudar-cuts-risk-recurrence-or-death-nearly-50  
**Collected:** 2026-06-07  
**Published:** ASCO GU 2026 (Abstract LBA630, presented Feb 27, 2026)

---

## Design

- Phase 3, open-label; NCT04700124
- N=808 (405 EV+pembro, 403 gemcitabine/cisplatin)
- Eligibility: T2-T4aN0M0 or **T1-T4aN1M0**; cisplatin-eligible; RC + PLND planned
- EV+pembro: 4 neoadjuvant cycles → RC → adjuvant EV (5 cycles) + pembro (13 cycles)
- Control: 4 cycles GC → RC → observation

## Primary results (median follow-up 33.6 mo)

| Endpoint | EV+pembro | GC | HR (95% CI) |
|----------|-----------|-----|-------------|
| 2-y EFS | 79.4% | 66.2% | 0.53 (0.41-0.70) |
| 2-y OS | 86.9% | 81.3% | 0.65 (0.48-0.89) |
| pCR | 55.8% | 32.5% | diff 23.4% |

- Median EFS: NR (EV+P) vs 48.5 mo (GC)
- Benefits consistent across pre-specified subgroups including **clinical stage**
- cN1 subgroup patient count **not yet reported** in public materials

## Regulatory

- FDA priority review sBLA (action date August 17, 2026)
- Would extend EV+pembro perioperative indication to cisplatin-eligible MIBC

**Patient match:** Direct perioperative EV+pembro in cisplatin-fit cN1 M0 — strongest new evidence for her profile if cisplatin-eligible.
