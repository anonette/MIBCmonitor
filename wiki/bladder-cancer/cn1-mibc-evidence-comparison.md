# Evidence Comparison: Perioperative Systemic Therapy for cN1 cM0 MIBC

**Updated:** 2026-06-06  
**Sources:** ClinicalTrials.gov; NEJM; ESMO/ASCO abstracts; EAU 2026; PMC reviews (2024–2026)  
**Raw:** (to be ingested into `raw/bladder-cancer/` on next agent run)

## Executive Summary

For **cT2 high-grade, L1, PET-positive external iliac node, cN1 cM0** bladder cancer with **curative intent (systemic therapy → cystectomy)**:

1. **EV-ECLIPSE** is the only prospective trial **designed specifically** for node-positive MIBC before surgery with EV+pembro (~23 patients planned; recruiting; no published efficacy yet).
2. **EV-302** provides the **strongest efficacy data** for EV+pembro vs chemotherapy, but in **unresectable/metastatic** disease; the closest subgroup is **lymph-node–only** (n=207, 23% of trial) — still not perioperative.
3. **NIAGARA** is the **strongest perioperative phase 3** evidence if she is **cisplatin-eligible** — but the **cN1 subgroup (n=58)** did **not** show statistically significant EFS benefit (HR 0.75, 95% CI 0.22–1.64).
4. **KEYNOTE-905** is definitive perioperative EV+pembro phase 3 data, but only **~17 patients (4.9%)** had cN1, and enrollment required **cisplatin-ineligibility** — a different population than cisplatin-fit standard NAC.
5. **SWOG 8710 / BA06 / NAC meta-analyses** — the Czech doctors' baseline — enrolled **cN0 only**; cN+ benefit is inferred from **retrospective series**, not these RCTs.

**Clean argument for the oncologist:** EV+pembro has powerful activity in urothelial cancer and a dedicated perioperative node-positive trial (EV-ECLIPSE), but **no phase 3 perioperative EV+pembro data in cisplatin-eligible cN1 cM0** yet. Against that, **NIAGARA is approved/reimbursable perioperative chemo-IO** with 58 cN1 patients (underpowered subgroup). **EV-302 LN-only (n=207)** supports biological activity but is the wrong clinical setting.

## Detailed Trial Notes

### A. EV + Pembrolizumab

#### 1. EV-ECLIPSE / MSK 21-316 (NCT05239624) — **Highest conceptual fit**

- **Eligibility:** cT2–T4 **N1–N3 M0** OR cT1 N2–N3 M0; MIBC confirmed on TURBT; node positivity by biopsy or imaging (≥1.0 cm short axis if no biopsy).
- **Treatment:** EV + pembrolizumab neoadjuvant → radical cystectomy → adjuvant pembrolizumab (11 cycles).
- **Enrollment:** ~23 patients (phase 2, recruiting since 2022).
- **Her match:** **Near-perfect** — cT2, cN1 (external iliac), cM0, cystectomy candidate. PET-positive node fits trial imaging criteria.
- **Evidence status:** No peer-reviewed efficacy as of June 2026. Primary endpoint is **pathologic complete response (pCR)**.

#### 2. EV-302 / KEYNOTE-A39 (NCT04223856) — **Strongest EV+pembro efficacy**

- **Population:** Previously untreated **locally advanced or metastatic** urothelial carcinoma; platinum-eligible.
- **N=886** randomized (EV+P 442 vs chemo 444).
- **Lymph-node–only subgroup:** **n=207 (23%)** — defined as metastatic disease confined to lymph nodes without visceral metastases.
  - ORR: 77.5% vs 53.4%
  - PFS HR 0.40 (95% CI 0.26–0.62)
  - OS HR 0.46 (95% CI 0.27–0.78)
- **Her match:** Partial — shares **node-only systemic disease** biology, but trial patients are **not curative perioperative** (unresectable or metastatic management, not planned NAC→RC sequence).

#### 3. EV-103 / KEYNOTE-869 Cohort K — **Accelerated-approval path**

- **N=76** received EV+P (cisplatin-ineligible la/mUC).
- cORR 64.5%; supported FDA approval pathway before EV-302.
- **Her match:** Low for perioperative argument; relevant only as tolerability/activity background.

#### 4. KEYNOTE-905 / EV-303 (NCT03924895) — **Perioperative phase 3 EV+pembro**

- **N=344** (cisplatin-ineligible or declined); T2–T4aN0M0 **or T1–T4aN1M0**.
- Baseline staging: T2N0 18%; T3/T4aN0 77%; **T1–T4aN1 4.9% (~17 patients)**.
- **Results (ESMO 2025):** EFS HR 0.40; OS HR 0.50; pCR 57.1% vs 8.6%.
- **Her match:** Partial — same **cN1 M0** stage allowed, but:
  - Trial population is **>80% cisplatin-ineligible** (median age ~74).
  - If she is **cisplatin-fit**, she does not match the studied population.
  - cN1 subgroup too small for reliable inference.

### B. Standard Chemotherapy Before Cystectomy

#### 5–7. SWOG 8710, BA06, Meta-analyses

| Study | N | cN+ enrolled? | Notes |
|-------|--:|:---:|---|
| SWOG 8710 | 307 | **No** | T2N0–T4aN0 only; MVAC NAC improved survival |
| BA06 30894 | ~976 | **No** | CMV NAC; N0 muscle-invasive |
| ABC meta-analysis | 3,005+ | Mostly N0 RCTs | ~5% absolute OS benefit at 5 years with NAC |
| Retrospective cN+ series (Zargar-Shoshtar et al.) | Multi-institutional cN1-3 | **Yes** | pCR 14.5%; pN0 rate 48% after NAC; median OS NR/45/14 mo by path stage |
| Population cN+ study (n=659) | cN+ MIBC | **Yes** | cN1: 3-y OS 66% NAC vs 37% upfront RC; pCD 39% vs 5% |

**Her match:** Czech standard is built on **N0 trials**; cN1 support is **retrospective**, not from SWOG 8710 itself.

### C. Perioperative Immunotherapy + Chemotherapy

#### 8. NIAGARA (NCT03732677) — **Approved perioperative alternative**

- **N=1,063** (durva+GC+adjuvant durva vs GC alone); cisplatin-eligible cT2–T4a **N0/1** M0.
- **cN1 subgroup: n=58** — EFS HR 0.75 (95% CI 0.22–1.64), **not statistically significant** (small n).
- Overall: 2-y EFS 67.8% vs 59.8% (HR 0.68); 2-y OS 82.2% vs 75.2% (HR 0.75).
- FDA approved Feb 2025; EAU **strong** recommendation for perioperative durva+chemo in cN0-1 M0.
- **Her match:** Good if **cisplatin-fit** — same clinical stage envelope; but **does not validate EV+pembro**, and **cN1 benefit unproven** in subgroup.

### D. Adjuvant Immunotherapy (If Surgery Still Shows High Risk)

#### 9. CheckMate 274 — **n=709**

- High-risk post-RC: pT3/T4a or **pN+** (or ypT2+ after NAC).
- **pN1: 143 patients (20%)**; any pN+: ~52%.
- DFS HR 0.70; benefit consistent across nodal status.
- **Role for her:** Relevant **after** cystectomy if pathology shows pN+ or residual muscle-invasive disease — backup plan, not primary neoadjuvant argument.

#### 10. AMBASSADOR — **n=702**

- Adjuvant pembrolizumab vs observation; high-risk post-surgery (≥ypT2, pN+, or positive margins).
- DFS benefit; OS not mature / confounded by crossover.
- **Role for her:** Adjuvant pembro option post-op; parallels CheckMate 274.

### E. Metastatic Standards (Weaker for Her Setting)

#### 11–12. JAVELIN 100, CheckMate 901

- Sequence: platinum → maintenance IO (avelumab) or nivo+chemo.
- Relevant to Czech doctors' **metastatic mental model**, not curative cN1 M0 perioperative plan.

## Recommended Argument Structure for the Clinical Conversation

### If she is **cisplatin-eligible** (likely given age/fitness unless contraindications):

| Line of argument | Evidence anchor | Patient n |
|------------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Standard of care | SWOG 8710 + meta-analyses + EAU | 0 cN1 in RCTs; retrospective cN+ data |
| Strongest **approved** perioperative IO | NIAGARA | 58 cN1 (subgroup NS) |
| EV+pembro **efficacy** | EV-302 | 207 LN-only (metastatic) |
| EV+pembro **perioperative cN1** | EV-ECLIPSE | ~23 (immature) |
| EV+pembro perioperative phase 3 | KEYNOTE-905 | ~17 cN1 — **wrong cisplatin status** |

**Honest bottom line:** Requesting EV+pembro pre-cystectomy in a **cisplatin-fit cN1** patient is **ahead of the evidence** relative to NIAGARA — unless she is cisplatin-ineligible (then KEYNOTE-905 becomes more relevant).

### If she is **cisplatin-ineligible**:

| Option | Evidence | cN1 n |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Perioperative EV+pembro | KEYNOTE-905 phase 3 | ~17 |
| Perioperative EV+pembro (node-focused) | EV-ECLIPSE phase 2 | ~23 |
| RC alone (old control) | KEYNOTE-905 control arm | included |

KEYNOTE-905 is the strongest **direct** EV+pembro perioperative data, but cN1 numbers remain tiny.

## Evidence Gaps (What the Agent Should Track)

1. **EV-ECLIPSE** primary results — expected ~2026–2027.
2. **KEYNOTE-B15 / EV-304** — cisplatin-**eligible** perioperative EV+pembro vs GC (NCT04700124; completion est. Dec 2026).
3. **KEYNOTE-905 cN1 subgroup** mature EFS/OS.
4. **NIAGARA cN1** longer follow-up.
5. Czech **reimbursement / SUKL** decisions for perioperative EV+pembro vs durvalumab.
